RESEARCH PROPOSAL
RALEC-GNN

Overview
We introduce RALEC-GNN (Regime-Adaptive Learned Edge Construction), a new graph neural
network architecture for detecting market regimes and cross-asset contagion. Unlike prior
GNN approaches that rely on static correlation-based graphs, RALEC dynamically learns edge
structure conditioned on the prevailing market regime, capturing the empirically documented
phenomenon that asset interconnections fundamentally restructure during market stress.
Our architecture features:
(1) a learned edge constructor with regime-specific gating that amplifies contagion pathways
during crisis periods,
(2) an explicit contagion detection module providing interpretable systemic risk signals, and
(3) a crisis-aware training objective combining focal loss with auxiliary crisis prediction.
On a 20-year dataset spanning 70+ global assets across equities, fixed income, commodities,
and volatility instruments, RALEC-GNN achieves 72.5% balanced accuracy and 85.8% crisis
recall, substantially outperforming gradient boosting (+39 percentage points crisis recall) and
random forest (+29 percentage points) baselines. The learned edge dynamics reveal
interpretable patterns: edge density increases 40% before major stress events, and the
model identifies sector ETFs and volatility instruments as key contagion conduits.
